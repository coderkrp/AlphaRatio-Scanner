# Scanner Logic & Rules

This document explains the quantitative logic behind AlphaRatio Scanner. Understanding these mechanics is crucial for trusting the alerts generated by the system.

## 1. The Core Concept: Relative Strength (Ratio)

Standard technical analysis looks at the absolute price of a stock (e.g., ₹1000). 
Ratio analysis looks at the stock's price divided by a benchmark's price.

**Formula:**
```text
Ratio = Asset_Close / Benchmark_Close
```

If `RELIANCE` is up 2% today, and `NIFTY50` is down 1%, the absolute price of Reliance looks okay, but the *Ratio* of Reliance vs Nifty will show a sharp upward slope, indicating **Alpha** (outperformance).

## 2. Calculated Metrics

For every defined timeframe (Daily, Weekly, Monthly), the engine computes:

### 2.1. Ratio 52-Week High Proximity (`rs_strength_pct`)
How close is the current Ratio to its highest point in the last 252 trading days?
- `rs_strength_pct = (Current_Ratio / Rolling_52W_High_Ratio) * 100`
- **Interpretation**: A value of `100%` means the stock is currently at a 52-week relative high against the benchmark. A value of `80%` means it has drawn down 20% compared to the benchmark.

### 2.2. All-Time High Proximity (`distance_to_ath_pct`)
How close is the current Ratio to its All-Time High?
- `distance_to_ath_pct = ((Current_Ratio - ATH_Ratio) / ATH_Ratio) * 100`
- **Interpretation**: A value of `0%` means it's breaking out to new ATHs. `-5%` means it is 5% away from its ATH breakout.

## 3. The Watchlist Engine & Hysteresis

The goal of the scanner is to alert the user when a stock becomes a "Leader". However, financial data is noisy. If we set a hard rule like "Alert when `rs_strength_pct` > 95%", a stock hovering around 94.9% and 95.1% will trigger an entry alert on Monday, an exit alert on Tuesday, and an entry alert on Wednesday. This is known as a **whipsaw**.

### Hysteresis Implementation

To solve this, AlphaRatio Scanner uses a state-machine with built-in hysteresis bands:

- **Entry Threshold**: `95%` (The ratio must reach within 5% of its 52-week high to enter the Watchlist).
- **Exit Threshold**: `85%` (The ratio must drop *significantly* below the highs to trigger a removal from the Watchlist).

**Example Scenario:**
1. **Day 1**: Stock RS is 92%. (State: Not in Watchlist).
2. **Day 5**: Stock RS hits 96%. (State: **ENTRY ALERT TRIGGERED**. Added to Watchlist).
3. **Day 10**: Stock RS drops to 91%. (State: Still in Watchlist. No alert. Tolerating normal pullback).
4. **Day 15**: Stock RS drops to 84%. (State: **EXIT ALERT TRIGGERED**. Removed from Watchlist).

This logic ensures that your Telegram is only pinged for meaningful, sustained changes in market leadership.

## 4. Multi-Timeframe Confirmation
The engines calculate these metrics independently across Daily, Weekly, and Monthly aggregated data. A true market leader will exhibit `rs_strength_pct > 95%` on the Daily chart, *and* be pushing toward ATHs on the Monthly chart. The Telegram alert formats this clearly so the trader can see timeframe alignment at a glance.
